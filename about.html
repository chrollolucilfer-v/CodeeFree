About the register

We index the world's free courses so you don't have to hunt for them.

CodeeFreee is an independent catalog of free courses and certificates from universities and learning platforms around the world — built for learners who'd rather compare options in one place than dig through forty different sites.

Our story

Free, high-quality courses from places like MIT, Harvard, and Google exist — but they're scattered across dozens of separate sites, each with its own search box, its own filters, and its own way of describing what "free" actually includes.

We started CodeeFreee to fix that: one register, pulled from trusted sources, kept up to date, so a learner can see at a glance whether a course is free to audit, offers a free certificate, or requires payment for the credential.

We're not a university and we don't issue certificates ourselves. Every course in the register links back to the original source — we just make the discovery part faster.

How the register is built
01

Source

We track course catalogs from universities and platforms that publish free, publicly accessible learning content.

02

Verify

Each entry is checked for accuracy — price, certificate availability, duration, and level — before it's added to the register.

03

Catalog

Courses are tagged by category, language, and difficulty so they surface correctly in search and filters.

04

Recheck

Listings are periodically revisited, since free courses occasionally move behind a paywall or get retired — we update the register when that happens.

What we optimize for
01

Accuracy over volume

A smaller, verified register beats a huge list full of dead links and outdated pricing.

02

No hidden costs

We always show whether a certificate is free or paid — never just "free course" with a paywall hiding behind it.

03

Source neutrality

We don't rank listings by who pays us. Sorting reflects popularity, rating, or recency — not sponsorship.

04

Point you onward

Every listing sends you to the original course page — we're a directory, not a replacement for the source.
